## What We Do

Healthified Preventive Medicine Services is a registered Nigerian NGO (CAC BN: 8264503) working to strengthen demand-side primary health care (PHC) in Lagos and across Africa.

We believe Universal Health Coverage starts with informed communities. Our work focuses on educating young adults about their PHC entitlements — so that community demand pressures providers and government toward real, measurable improvement.

**Theory of change:** Informed young adults → community demand → provider and government accountability.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Only **21% of Lagos PHC catchment population** used a PHC in 2025 (LSPHCB Annual Activity Report, 2025).

Three validated barriers:
1. **Unawareness** — most residents don't know a PHC exists near them
2. **Identity confusion** — "PHC is only for antenatal care"
3. **Quality perception** — communities distrust PHC services before ever using them

These barriers are demand-side. They are solvable through education.

## Programs

### 🏥 CHW Volunteer Training Program
A 12-week WhatsApp-based Community Health Worker training program using the Johns Hopkins CHW Coursera curriculum as backbone, adapted through the SPARK Model (Set the stage, Point of learning, Analogy/story, Reflect, Key takeaway).

- 24 sessions delivered via WhatsApp (Mon/Thu, 5–6 PM WAT)
- Mixed health and non-health background volunteers
- Designed to be replicable by other NGOs and PHCs across Nigeria

### 🏫 HealthRoots School Health Program
Monthly school health outreach targeting Lagos secondary school students — bringing preventive health education directly into schools.

### 📱 PHC Strengthening Content
Free social media health education content (Instagram, Twitter/X, WhatsApp) designed to pass the **"Amaka Test"** — would a 20–26 year old urban Lagos woman save it, send it to her mum, or search for her nearest PHC?

## Why This Matters

Nigeria has over 30,000 PHCs. The majority are underutilised — not because they don't exist, but because communities don't know what they offer, don't trust them, or don't identify as people who should use them.

This repository is infrastructure for fixing that — freely available, community-validated, and built for the Nigerian urban context.
