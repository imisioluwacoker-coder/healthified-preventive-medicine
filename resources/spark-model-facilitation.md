## Why SPARK?

Most community health training materials are written for classroom delivery — slide decks, printed handouts, in-person lectures. These formats don't work for:
- Volunteers with full-time jobs or school schedules
- Communities with unreliable power or data costs
- Urban Nigeria, where people absorb information on their phones, in transit, between obligations

The SPARK Model was developed by Healthified to deliver structured, evidence-based health education through WhatsApp — in short, engaging bursts that respect volunteers' time and bandwidth.

It is freely available for adaptation.

---

## The Five Stages

### S — Set the Stage
**Goal:** Activate what the learner already knows. Reduce anxiety. Make the topic feel relevant to their real life.

**How to do it:**
- Start with a question, not a fact
- Reference a familiar experience or common scenario in their community
- Keep it to 2–3 sentences maximum

**Example (Hypertension session):**
> "Before we start today — raise your hand (or drop a 🙋) if someone in your family has ever been told their blood pressure is high. Now keep your hand up if that person took their medication every day. Notice anything?"

---

### P — Point of Learning
**Goal:** Deliver the core concept. One idea. Clearly. Simply.

**How to do it:**
- State the key fact or concept in plain language
- Avoid clinical jargon — if you must use a term, define it immediately
- One paragraph maximum

**Example:**
> "Hypertension — high blood pressure — is called the silent killer because it causes no pain. You can have dangerously high blood pressure right now and feel completely fine. The only way to know is to check it. That's what makes it so deadly — and so preventable."

---

### A — Analogy or Story
**Goal:** Make the concept stick. Ground it in something tangible.

**How to do it:**
- Use a Nigerian context — Lagos traffic, market dynamics, family situations
- Tell a short story (real or composite — never fabricated data)
- Or use a metaphor that maps to the concept

**Example:**
> "Think of your blood vessels like the water pipes under your street. When pressure builds up too high and there's nowhere for it to go — something bursts. That's what a stroke is. Hypertension is the slow, invisible pressure building in those pipes every day."

---

### R — Reflect
**Goal:** Give the learner a moment to process. Invite engagement.

**How to do it:**
- Ask one question — not multiple
- Make it easy to answer (emoji reaction, one word, or short sentence)
- Don't rush past this stage

**Example:**
> "So here's a question for the group: When did you or someone in your household last have their blood pressure checked? Drop your answer — even if it's 'never' or 'I don't know.' No judgment."

---

### K — Key Takeaway
**Goal:** One clear, actionable truth they carry forward.

**How to do it:**
- Write it as a single sentence
- Make it actionable, not just informational
- Make it shareable — the kind of thing they'd send to a family WhatsApp group

**Example:**
> "**Today's takeaway:** High blood pressure has no symptoms. The only way to know your number is to check it — and your nearest PHC will do it for free."

## Timing Guide (WhatsApp Format)

| Stage | Recommended Length |
|---|---|
| Set the stage | 2–3 sentences |
| Point of learning | 1 short paragraph |
| Analogy / Story | 3–5 sentences |
| Reflect | 1 question + wait 5–10 mins |
| Key takeaway | 1 sentence, bolded |
| **Total session time** | **45–60 minutes including discussion** |

---

## Facilitator Tips

- **Send each stage as a separate message** — not one long wall of text
- **Use voice notes** for the Analogy/Story stage when possible — storytelling lands better in audio
- **Don't skip the Reflect stage** — the discussion it generates is often more valuable than the content itself
- **Read the room** — if the group is quiet, ask a direct follow-up. Silence doesn't always mean understanding
- **Prepare your session guide 48 hours early** — it gives you time to test the analogy and anticipate questions

---

## Adapting SPARK for Your Context

SPARK was designed for Nigerian urban contexts but the structure is transferable. If you are adapting it:
- Keep the Analogy/Story stage culturally grounded for your community
- Adjust timing based on your group's engagement patterns
- The model works for in-person, WhatsApp, Telegram, or any messaging platform
