## Program Overview

A 12-week WhatsApp-based CHW training program designed to equip community volunteers — with and without health backgrounds — to deliver basic preventive health education in their communities.

**Duration:** 12 weeks | 24 sessions
**Delivery:** WhatsApp voice notes, text messages, and discussion prompts
**Schedule:** Monday and Thursday, 5:00–6:00 PM (WAT)
**Curriculum backbone:** Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health — CHW Training (Coursera)

---

## Who It's For

This program is designed for:
- Young adults with no formal health training
- Community volunteers who want to serve their neighbourhoods
- NGO volunteers being equipped for health outreach roles
- Individuals interested in PHC advocacy and community mobilisation

No health background is required.

---

## The SPARK Model

All sessions are facilitated using the **SPARK Model** — a structured approach to adult learning in low-bandwidth, mobile-first environments:

| Letter | Stage | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Set the stage | Activate prior knowledge; connect to lived experience |
| **P** | Point of learning | Introduce the core concept clearly and simply |
| **A** | Analogy / Story | Ground the concept in a relatable Nigerian context |
| **R** | Reflect | Prompt volunteers to process and respond |
| **K** | Key takeaway | One clear, memorable action or truth to carry forward |

## 12-Week Curriculum Structure

### Module 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–2)
- What is primary health care?
- The PHC package in Nigeria: what it includes and who it's for
- Understanding Universal Health Coverage (UHC)
- The community health worker's role

### Module 2 — Common Preventable Conditions (Weeks 3–5)
- Hypertension: silent, common, preventable
- Diabetes awareness and screening
- Malaria prevention and early care-seeking
- Reproductive and maternal health basics

### Module 3 — Community Outreach Skills (Weeks 6–8)
- Health communication fundamentals
- Running a basic screening station
- How to refer community members to PHC services
- Documentation and data capture basics

### Module 4 — Adolescent and Youth Health (Weeks 9–10)
- AYFS: Adolescent and Youth-Friendly Services at PHCs
- Mental health awareness for young people
- Substance use and tobacco: brief intervention (Ask-Advise-Refer model)

### Module 5 — Advocacy and Accountability (Weeks 11–12)
- Community rights and PHC entitlements
- How to advocate for PHC quality improvement
- Documenting and reporting community health needs
- Graduation and next steps

## Facilitator Notes

- Session guides are prepared and distributed to lead facilitators 48 hours in advance
- Research materials are translated into accessible, conversational session messages — not dropped as papers into the group
- Volunteers are encouraged to share session content within their own family and community networks
- Discussion prompts close each session to drive reflection and peer learning
