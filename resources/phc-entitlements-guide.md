## The Problem

Most Nigerians — especially young adults in urban areas — do not know:
1. That a Primary Health Care (PHC) centre likely exists within walking distance of where they live
2. That most PHC services are **free or heavily subsidised** under the Basic Health Care Provision Fund (BHCPF)
3. That PHCs offer far more than antenatal care

Only **21% of Lagos PHC catchment populations** used a PHC in 2025 (LSPHCB Annual Activity Report).

This is a knowledge gap — not a service gap.

---

## What PHC Is

A Primary Health Care (PHC) centre is your **first point of contact** with the formal health system. It is government-run, community-based, and designed to handle the majority of health needs before they become emergencies.

PHC is **not only for pregnant women.** It is for everyone.

---

## The PHC Service Package

Under Nigeria's revised Basic Health Care Provision Fund (BHCPF), your local PHC should offer:

### Preventive Services (Often Free)
- Blood pressure screening
- Blood sugar (diabetes) screening
- Malaria testing and treatment
- HIV counselling and testing
- Tuberculosis (TB) screening
- Family planning counselling and services
- Immunisation (children and adults)
- Antenatal and postnatal care

### Curative Services
- Treatment of common illnesses (fever, cough, infections, diarrhoea)
- Wound care and minor procedures
- Referral letters to secondary/tertiary hospitals when needed

### Adolescent and Youth-Friendly Services (AYFS)
This is a **documented but severely underutilised** service package for individuals aged 10–24. It includes:
- Confidential reproductive health services
- Mental health first contact
- Substance use screening and brief counselling
- Nutritional counselling

Most young people have never heard of AYFS — even though it exists specifically for them.

## How to Find Your Nearest PHC

1. Ask a neighbour or community leader — PHCs are usually within your local government ward
2. Search "[Your Local Government Area] primary health centre Lagos" on Google Maps
3. Ask at your LCDA (Local Council Development Area) office
4. Contact the Lagos State Primary Health Care Board (LSPHCB) via their official channels

---

## Your Rights as a PHC User

- You have the right to be **seen and assessed**, not turned away
- You have the right to **referral** if your condition requires a higher level of care
- You have the right to **dignified treatment** regardless of your ability to pay
- You have the right to **information** about your health condition and treatment options

## Why This Matters for Your Family

The leading causes of preventable death in Nigeria — hypertension, diabetes complications, cervical cancer, prostate cancer, malaria — can be **detected early at a PHC**.
